# Results on the Table Plan — Libyan Safe Residency

## Decision

The frontend demo is considered **done** and will not be the main object from now on.

The active work now becomes: **bring results to the table** — prepare the strongest business-facing evidence, diagnosis, and decision package for Libyan Safe Residency.

This is not a plan to keep polishing the demo.  
This is a plan to convert what we learned into a clear conversation with the business stakeholders.

---

## What “results on the table” means

The goal is to walk into the next discussion with a compact package that shows:

1. What is happening now.
2. Where the business is leaking value.
3. What evidence supports that diagnosis.
4. What decisions the team must make.
5. What the first practical operating change should be.
6. What should be measured after that.

The output should be understandable by Ayoub and the technical employee without needing to inspect the demo code.

---

## Primary message

Libyan Safe Residency does not have a demand problem.

It has an intake-control and follow-up visibility problem.

People are already reaching out through TikTok/Facebook, but serious leads can disappear inside inboxes, manual replies, spreadsheets, memory, and paper/cash processes.

The business result to pursue is not “build an app.”  
The business result is:

> Fewer missed qualified clients, faster qualification, clearer follow-up, and one reliable view of case status.

---

## Stakeholder table package

Prepare these 7 artifacts.

### 1. One-page executive summary

Purpose: explain the situation in one page.

Content:

- Current demand source: TikTok first, Facebook also present.
- Current operating method: manual inbox replies, Gemini manually, Google Sheets/Excel, paper documents, cash deposit, memory.
- Main risk: qualified clients are missed or delayed before becoming tracked cases.
- Core gap: no strong operational layer between social inbox and case follow-up.
- Business result target: capture and organize demand already coming in.

Recommended title:

**من رسائل السوشيال إلى حالات قابلة للمتابعة**

### 2. Current flow map

Purpose: make the current process visible.

Flow:

TikTok/Facebook message → manual reply → manual questions → eligibility guess/review → papers/passport requested → cash deposit → paperwork → status update in sheet/memory → completion/follow-up.

Highlight weak points:

- Lead can remain only in inbox.
- Qualification questions may vary by team member.
- Sheet may not reflect real status.
- Deposit/document status may be remembered manually.
- Client may not know next step.

### 3. Lead qualification table

Purpose: define how inquiries should be separated before solution discussion.

Categories:

- Preliminarily qualified.
- Missing information.
- Missing documents.
- Not able to start currently.
- Requires manual review.

For each category, define:

- What it means.
- What next action is needed.
- Who should handle it.
- Whether the client should be followed up later.

### 4. Risk/value table

Purpose: show why this matters commercially.

Rows:

- Missed qualified clients.
- Slow response.
- Repeated explanation.
- Unclear case status.
- Weak payment/document visibility.
- Team dependency on memory.
- Client uncertainty.

For each row:

- Current cause.
- Business impact.
- What evidence to collect.

### 5. Minimum operating rules

Purpose: agree on behavior before choosing software.

Rules:

1. Every serious TikTok/Facebook inquiry must be recorded outside the inbox.
2. Every recorded lead must have one owner.
3. Every lead must have one clear status.
4. Every lead must have a next action.
5. Payment status must be separate from eligibility status.
6. Document status must be separate from payment status.
7. No payment request before preliminary review.
8. Sensitive/passport data must be handled carefully and only when needed.

### 6. Measurement plan

Purpose: define results, not features.

Track weekly:

- Number of new social inquiries.
- Number recorded as leads/cases.
- Number preliminarily qualified.
- Number missing documents/info.
- Number not currently able to start.
- Number needing manual review.
- Number followed up within 24 hours.
- Number that delivered documents/passport.
- Number that paid deposit.
- Number under process.
- Number completed.
- Number lost/no response.

Key ratios:

- Inquiry → recorded lead conversion.
- Recorded lead → qualified conversion.
- Qualified → documents delivered.
- Documents delivered → deposit received.
- Under process → completed.
- Average response/follow-up delay.

### 7. Decision sheet

Purpose: force the next meeting to produce decisions.

Questions to answer:

1. Who owns TikTok inquiry capture daily?
2. Who owns case review?
3. What minimum fields must be recorded for every lead?
4. What statuses will the team actually use?
5. What documents are checked before saying “ready to proceed”?
6. When exactly is the deposit requested?
7. Who records cash deposit received?
8. What client message should be sent for each status?
9. What should never be written in a client-facing status?
10. What is the first 2-week operational target?

## Meeting structure

### Meeting goal

Agree on the first operational result to improve, not on a big software build.

### Suggested agenda — 60 minutes

1. **5 min — Current reality**
   - Demand exists.
   - Inbox is not enough.

2. **10 min — Flow map**
   - Walk through how a real TikTok lead becomes a case today.

3. **10 min — Risk/value table**
   - Identify where money/time is being lost.

4. **10 min — Qualification categories**
   - Agree on the status categories the team can actually use.

5. **10 min — Minimum operating rules**
   - Agree on who records what and when.

6. **10 min — Measurement plan**
   - Pick 5 weekly numbers to track first.

7. **5 min — Next action**
   - Assign owner and timeline for the first operating change.

---

## Recommended first result target

For the first 2 weeks, do not target “full system launch.”

Target this result:

> 100% of serious TikTok/Facebook inquiries are captured into one structured lead list with owner, status, next action, document state, and follow-up date.

Why this target:

- It attacks the biggest risk directly: missed qualified clients.
- It does not require changing the whole business.
- It creates measurable proof quickly.
- It reveals the real volume and bottlenecks.

---

## First 5 metrics to put on the table

If the team can only track five numbers first, track these:

1. New TikTok/Facebook inquiries this week.
2. Inquiries captured into the structured list.
3. Preliminarily qualified leads.
4. Leads waiting on documents/info.
5. Leads followed up within 24 hours.

These five numbers are enough to reveal whether the business is controlling demand or still losing it inside inboxes.

---

## What not to discuss first

Avoid leading with:

- full app build
- backend architecture
- payment integration
- AI automation
- TikTok/Facebook API integration
- advanced roles/permissions
- document upload systems

Those can come later.

First, get agreement on the operational result and the status discipline.

## Success definition

This phase is successful when Ayoub and the technical employee can clearly answer:

- Which leads are serious?
- Which serious leads were captured?
- Which ones need documents or info?
- Which ones are ready for review?
- Which ones paid deposit?
- Which ones are under process?
- Who owns the next action?
- What changed this week compared with last week?

If those questions can be answered reliably, Libyan Safe Residency has moved from scattered inbox demand toward controlled operations.
